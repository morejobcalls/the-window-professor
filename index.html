Meet the Owner

He was a teacher
before he was a contractor.

"I'm not a salesman. I never have been. My job is to help you understand what you're buying and why it's right for your home."

Before Jeff Haring ever measured a window or swung a hammer, he stood in front of a classroom. When he made the move into home improvement, he brought the one thing almost nobody in the trades ever does: the instinct to teach first and sell second.

Homeowners started calling him "the professor" not because it was on a truck door, but because sitting across from Jeff at a kitchen table felt different from every other contractor they'd met. He explained product grades. He told them what questions to ask competitors. He was honest about what their home needed — and what it didn't.

That philosophy built a company from the ground up — and still drives every estimate, every install, and every follow-up call today.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is the best window replacement company in Oakland County, Michigan?

The Window Professor is a top-rated window replacement contractor serving all of Oakland County. We specialize in premium window installation and replacement for residential homes — serving Clarkston, Rochester Hills, Auburn Hills, Milford, White Lake Township, Waterford, Commerce Township, and Bloomfield Hills.

Does The Window Professor serve Livingston County, Michigan?

Yes. We serve all of Livingston County — including Brighton, Howell, Hartland, Pinckney, Hamburg Township, and Genoa Township — as well as Genesee County, including Grand Blanc, Fenton, Davison, and surrounding areas. Window replacement, siding installation, and custom deck construction are available throughout all three counties.

Does The Window Professor install siding?

Yes. Siding installation and replacement is one of our core services. We install vinyl, fiber cement, board & batten, and engineered wood siding for Michigan homeowners. Whether you're replacing worn-out siding or giving your home a complete exterior refresh, we handle the full project from removal to finish.

What siding materials do you recommend for Michigan homes?

For Michigan's climate, we recommend fiber cement siding (like James Hardie) for maximum durability — it resists moisture, insects, and freeze-thaw cycles without warping or rotting. Premium vinyl siding is an excellent lower-maintenance option. For a modern look, board & batten profiles are increasingly popular. We'll help you choose the right material for your home and budget.

What deck materials do you recommend for Michigan winters?

For Michigan's freeze-thaw cycles, we recommend Wolf Decking composite for its durability and low maintenance. Wolf composite resists moisture, warping, and cracking — common issues with pressure-treated wood. We also work with premium hardwood and PVC depending on the project.

How long does window replacement take?

Most window replacement projects are completed in a single day. A full home of 10–15 windows typically takes one to two days. Complex installs — bay windows, egress windows, or large picture windows — may require additional time. We provide a clear schedule with every quote.

Is The Window Professor licensed and insured in Michigan?

Yes. We are fully licensed and insured to operate as a residential contractor in Michigan. All work is performed by experienced installation crews and backed by a warranty on both materials and labor.

Do you offer free estimates?

Yes. We offer free, no-obligation project estimates to homeowners throughout Oakland County and Livingston County. Use the contact form below or call us directly to schedule.

How much does window replacement cost in Michigan?

Window replacement in Michigan typically ranges from $400 to $1,000 per window depending on type, size, and material. Full-home replacement projects vary widely based on scope. We provide transparent, itemized quotes with no hidden fees — contact us for a free estimate specific to your home.
