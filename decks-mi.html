Common Questions

Deck Construction FAQ — Michigan Homeowners

What decking material do you recommend for Michigan?

We recommend Wolf Decking composite for Michigan homeowners. Wolf composite resists moisture, warping, cracking, and the freeze-thaw damage that deteriorates pressure-treated wood over time. It's virtually maintenance-free — no annual staining or sealing — and backed by a strong manufacturer warranty.

Who builds custom decks in Oakland County, Michigan?

The Window Professor designs and builds custom decks throughout Oakland County — serving Clarkston, Rochester Hills, Milford, White Lake Township, Waterford, Auburn Hills, Commerce Township, and surrounding areas. We also serve all of Livingston County and Genesee County.

Do I need a permit to build a deck in Michigan?

Yes — in most Michigan municipalities, a permit is required for any deck that is attached to the home or over a certain height. We handle the permit application, scheduling, and inspections for you as part of every project. Your deck will be fully code-compliant.

How long does deck construction take?

A standard single-level deck typically takes three to five days from groundbreaking to final board. Multi-level decks, covered structures, or those requiring significant site prep may take one to two weeks. We provide a detailed project schedule with every quote.

How much does a deck cost in Michigan?

Deck costs vary based on size, material, and complexity. A standard composite deck typically ranges from $15,000 to $40,000 depending on square footage, railing system, and features. We provide transparent, itemized quotes with no hidden fees — contact us for a free estimate specific to your home and yard.

Do you also do windows and siding?

Yes. The Window Professor offers three core services: window replacement and installation, siding installation and replacement, and custom deck design and construction. Many homeowners bundle multiple projects — contact us to discuss your full scope and we'll provide a combined estimate.
