Most Popular

Double-Hung Windows

The most common window style for Michigan homes — both sashes slide up and down, making them easy to clean from inside and ideal for any room. Available in a wide range of sizes and finishes from Pella and ProVia.

  • Top and bottom sash operation
  • Easy interior cleaning
  • Available in vinyl, wood, and fiberglass
  • Energy-efficient glass packages
  • Fits standard and oversized openings

Casement & Awning Windows

Casement windows crank open outward for maximum airflow and a tight seal when closed — one of the most energy-efficient window styles available. Awning windows hinge at the top and are perfect for letting in air even in light rain.

  • Crank-out operation for full ventilation
  • Tight compression seal when closed
  • Ideal for hard-to-reach areas
  • Available in wide widths for views
  • Pella Impervia fiberglass option

Picture & Egress Windows

Picture windows maximize natural light and unobstructed views — perfect for great rooms and sunrooms. Egress windows are required by Michigan building code for finished basements and provide a critical emergency exit.

Window Replacement FAQ — Michigan Homeowners

We install Pella and ProVia windows exclusively. Both brands offer exceptional quality, energy performance, and warranty coverage built for Michigan's demanding climate. During your free estimate, we'll walk through the specific product lines that fit your home and budget.

Who installs windows in Oakland County, Michigan?

The Window Professor installs and replaces windows throughout Oakland County — serving Clarkston, Rochester Hills, Milford, White Lake Township, Waterford, Auburn Hills, Commerce Township, and surrounding areas. We also serve all of Livingston County and Genesee County.

How long does window replacement take?

Most window replacement projects are completed in a single day. A full home of 10–15 windows typically takes one to two days. Complex installs — bay windows, egress windows, or large picture windows — may require additional time. We provide a clear schedule with every quote.

What is the best window for Michigan winters?

For Michigan's freeze-thaw cycles, we recommend double or triple-pane windows with low-E glass coatings and inert gas fills (argon or krypton). Both Pella and ProVia offer window lines specifically engineered for cold climates. We'll help you choose the right glass package for your home's orientation and energy goals.

How much does window replacement cost in Michigan?

Window replacement costs typically range from $400 to $1,200 per window depending on the style, size, material, and glass package. Full-home replacement projects vary widely based on scope. We provide transparent, itemized quotes with no hidden fees — contact us for a free estimate specific to your home.
